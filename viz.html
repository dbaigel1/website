While living in China for the past year, I haven't had easy access to Western news sources. After reading some Chinese news, I began wondering whether or not media companies across the Atlantic operated in similar ways. I thought it would be interesting to see how subjective each source is on average, what topics they report on most often, and how polarized their language choice is. I targeted eight different news sources and gathered front page headlines from them over a period of a few months. The following are a few graphs that depict the data I gathered.

This chart shows how polar or subjective the average headline from a given news source is. Polarity is how polarized the speech is (-1 being the most negative while 1 being the most positive). Subjectivity is how factual the headline is (0 being an indisputable truth, while 1 being completely made up). The small circle located at 0 polarity and 0 subjectivity shows where a completely non-polar non-subjective news source would lie on the graph. Hover over any piece of data to learn more about it.
